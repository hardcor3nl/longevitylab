## Why omega-3s are a Tier 1 longevity supplement

EPA and DHA are the marine omega-3 fatty acids with the strongest evidence in longevity and cardiovascular medicine. They lower triglycerides, shift eicosanoid balance away from chronic inflammation, and support brain membrane structure.

Key signals:

- **VITAL** and related large trials support cardiovascular benefit especially when baseline fish intake is low
- **REDUCE-IT** (prescription icosapent ethyl) showed large event reduction in high-risk patients at high EPA dose — not the same product as OTC fish oil, but it validates EPA as a real lever
- Brain DHA stores track with cognitive resilience metrics in observational work

In our [complete longevity stack](/best/complete-longevity-stack), omega-3 sits in **Tier 1** with vitamin D, magnesium, creatine, and taurine.

## The purity problem (why brand matters more here)

Consumer Lab and IFOS testing regularly find that fish oil products can contain:

- **Oxidised lipids** — rancid oil that may be counterproductive
- **Label inaccuracy** — less EPA/DHA than claimed
- **Heavy metal contamination** — more common in uncertified budget oils

That is why third-party testing (IFOS, Friend of the Sea, USP) matters more for fish oil than for creatine monohydrate.

## How we rank fish oil

| Criterion | What we want |
|---|---|
| EPA + DHA per serving | Enough to hit 1–3g combined without 8 pills |
| Form | Triglyceride / re-esterified preferred for absorption |
| Oxidation markers | Low TOTOX; recent IFOS batch preferred |
| Contaminants | Heavy metals and PCBs within strict limits |
| Cost per gram EPA+DHA | Real value after serving size |

## Dose targets (practical)

| Goal | Combined EPA+DHA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General longevity / low fish diet | 1–2g/day | Most adults |
| High triglycerides (with clinician) | 2–4g/day | Medical supervision for high dose |
| Cognitive focus | Include ≥1g DHA | Food + supplement total |

**Timing:** with a fat-containing meal. **Storage:** cool, dark; discard if smell is strongly rancid.

## Who should prioritise omega-3 first

- Low fish intake (&lt;2 fatty fish meals/week)
- Elevated triglycerides or high inflammatory burden
- Plant-based diets (consider algae DHA + EPA sources)
- Cognitive / cardiovascular longevity focus after sleep and training basics

## Who should not self-dose aggressively

- People on anticoagulants without clinician input
- Upcoming surgery (discuss with surgeon)
- Unexplained bleeding history
