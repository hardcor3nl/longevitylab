## Why NMN?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It's essential for energy metabolism, DNA repair, and cellular signalling. The problem? NAD+ levels decline roughly **50% by age 50**.

NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) is a direct precursor to NAD+. Unlike NAD+ itself, NMN can cross cell membranes, making it an effective oral supplement.

## What the Science Says

David Sinclair's lab at Harvard has published extensively on NAD+ precursors. Key findings:

- **Washington University (2021):** 250mg NMN daily for 10 weeks improved muscle insulin sensitivity in postmenopausal women
- **Keio University (2022):** 250mg NMN for 12 weeks improved grip strength and walking speed in older adults
- **Meta-analysis (2023):** Across 8 RCTs, NMN supplementation significantly raised NAD+ blood levels

The consensus: NMN works to raise NAD+. Whether that translates to meaningful longevity benefits in humans is still under investigation — but the mechanistic case is strong.
