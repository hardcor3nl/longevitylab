## Why Irradiance Is the Only Spec That Matters

The red light therapy market is flooded with panels claiming impressive wattages, LED counts, and coverage areas. Most of these numbers are meaningless marketing. The one specification that determines whether a panel delivers therapeutic doses is **irradiance** — the amount of light energy hitting your skin per unit area, measured in milliwatts per square centimetre (mW/cm²).

The therapeutic dose for red light therapy is expressed as **energy density** (joules per cm²), calculated as:

**Irradiance (mW/cm²) × Time (seconds) = Energy Density (J/cm²)**

Target therapeutic doses for most applications fall between 10 and 40 J/cm². A panel delivering 50 mW/cm² needs 200–800 seconds (3–13 minutes) to reach that dose. A panel delivering 100 mW/cm² needs half the time — 100–400 seconds (1.5–7 minutes).

Cheap panels claiming "1500 watts" but delivering 30 mW/cm² at treatment distance require 5–22 minutes per area to achieve the same dose a quality panel delivers in under 5 minutes. Your time is limited. Irradiance matters.

## Dual-Chip Technology: Why It Matters

The MitoPRO 1500 uses dual-chip LEDs — each physical LED houses both a 660nm (red) emitter and an 850nm (near-infrared) emitter in a single chip package.

Competing panels often use alternating rows of red and NIR LEDs. While functional, this creates subtle striping in the irradiance pattern — areas slightly richer in red or NIR depending on your distance and position.

Dual-chip ensures **pixel-perfect mixing** of both wavelengths at every point in the treatment area. For most users this is academic, but for systematic facial or targeted treatment where positioning is precise, it guarantees uniform dose delivery.

- **6 inches:** Maximum irradiance — appropriate for body, joint, and muscle treatments

- **5–10 minutes per area** delivers 29–58 J/cm² — within the therapeutic window for most applications

### Frequency
- **3–5 sessions per week** for therapeutic goals (muscle recovery, skin, joint pain)
- Daily use is safe but benefits plateau without rest days for some applications

### Timing
- **Morning:** For energy, cognitive benefits, circadian alignment
- **Post-workout:** For muscle recovery and inflammation reduction
- **Avoid within 2 hours of sleep:** High-intensity red light can suppress melatonin in some individuals

### Eye Safety
- Never look directly at the LEDs
- Closing eyes during facial treatment is adequate for most people
- Goggles are optional but recommended for those who are photosensitive
