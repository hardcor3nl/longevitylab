## Why EMF Matters in a Sauna

Infrared saunas contain multiple electrical components in close proximity to your body for extended periods: heater panels, wiring, power supplies, and lighting. During a 30–45 minute session, you are sitting 12–18 inches from these components — closer and longer than virtually any other electrical device in daily life.

For most people, standard sauna EMF levels (2–5 mG at seated position) are not a significant concern based on current epidemiological evidence. But for:
- EMF-sensitive individuals who report physiological symptoms from exposure
- People with implanted medical devices where electromagnetic fields are contraindicated
- Those spending 5 or more sessions per week in the sauna for years
- Physicians prescribing sauna for therapeutic use who want to minimise every variable

...the lowest possible EMF is a legitimate priority. The Clearlight Sanctuary 2 is the definitive answer.

## True Wave II Heaters: The Technical Foundation

Clearlight's proprietary True Wave II heaters combine carbon and ceramic elements in a single heater panel. This combination achieves two things simultaneously that neither material accomplishes alone:

**Carbon elements** emit a broad far-infrared wavelength distribution with even heat coverage across the panel face. Carbon heaters warm up quickly — within 10–15 minutes to therapeutic temperature — and maintain even heat without the hot spots characteristic of older ceramic-only designs.

**Ceramic elements** emit at higher intensity in the therapeutic 9–10 micron peak of far-infrared — the wavelength range most readily absorbed by the water molecules in human tissue. Ceramic heaters historically ran hotter with less even coverage, but when combined with carbon in the True Wave II configuration, each element compensates for the other's weaknesses.

The result is even, intense, therapeutic far-infrared with the full-body coverage of carbon and the biologically optimal wavelength peak of ceramic — genuinely greater than the sum of its parts.

## Western Red Cedar: More Than Aesthetics

The Clearlight Sanctuary 2 is constructed from sustainably sourced Western red cedar — a choice that reflects both quality and practical engineering:

**Antimicrobial properties:** Cedar contains natural thujaplicins — tropolone-based antimicrobial compounds that inhibit mould, mildew, and bacterial growth. In a warm, humid environment cycled through repeatedly, this is hygienically meaningful. Budget saunas built from hemlock or unspecified "Chinese poplar" require more aggressive cleaning protocols and are more prone to mould colonisation in humid climates.

**Dimensional stability:** Cedar expands and contracts less with humidity changes than most softwoods. Over years of cycling between 120°F+ operating temperature and room temperature, this means fewer cracks, fewer gaps at panel joints, and better thermal seal integrity. Gaps in the sauna shell reduce both efficiency and the therapeutic steam-retention effect.

**Scent:** Fresh Western red cedar releases aromatic terpenes when heated — particularly cedrol and alpha-cedrene. The pleasant aroma is not merely sensory; cedrol has documented mild sedative and anxiolytic effects in animal and small human studies. The aromatherapy dimension of cedar sauna use has genuine if modest scientific support.

**Longevity:** Clearlight offers a lifetime warranty on both heaters and cedar construction — a commercial commitment only possible because these materials genuinely last. We have inspected 20-year-old cedar saunas in excellent structural condition. The economics of a lifetime warranty sauna versus a budget sauna replaced every 7–10 years shift the value calculation significantly.
