## How we rank longevity supplements

We score each compound on four criteria:

1. **Mechanistic plausibility** — does the biology make sense for aging pathways?
2. **Human RCT evidence** — trials in humans, not only mice
3. **Safety profile** — long-term risk and monitoring needs
4. **Effect size** — is the benefit meaningful outside a lab?

This is a **catalogue of best compounds**, not a shopping list to buy all at once. For doses, timing, and monthly cost, use the [complete longevity stack](/best/complete-longevity-stack). For personalised order of operations, start at [Build My Plan](/get-started).

## The list (evidence-first)

### 1. Omega-3 (EPA + DHA) — 88/100

Strongest cardiovascular longevity signal among over-the-counter options. REDUCE-IT and related work support high-dose EPA in high-risk groups; broader populations benefit most when baseline intake is low.

**Deep dive:** [Best omega-3 fish oil](/supplements/best-omega-3-fish-oil)  
**Database:** [Carlson / omega-3 card](/database/omega3-carlson)

### 2. Creatine monohydrate — 86/100

Hundreds of trials for strength; growing data for cognition and muscle preservation with age. Cheap, safe, boring — and that is the point.

**Deep dive:** [Best creatine supplements](/supplements/best-creatine-supplements)  
**Database:** [Creatine mono](/database/creatine-mono)

### 3. Vitamin D3 + K2 — 84/100

Deficiency is common; repletion is one of the highest ROI interventions when 25-OH D is low. Pair with K2 (MK-7) when using higher D3 doses.

**Deep dive:** [Best vitamin D3 + K2](/supplements/best-vitamin-d3-k2)  
**Database:** [Vitamin D3+K2](/database/vitamin-d3-k2)

### 4. Magnesium (glycinate / threonate) — 82/100

Cofactor in 300+ reactions; sleep and insulin sensitivity are the practical wins. Form matters more than brand marketing.

**Deep dive:** [Best magnesium supplements](/supplements/best-magnesium-supplements)  
**Database:** [Magnesium glycinate](/database/magnesium-glycinate)

### 5. NMN / NR (NAD+ precursors) — 82/100

Reliably raise NAD+ in humans; functional outcomes are promising but still maturing. Buy purity and dose transparency, not influencer labels.

**Deep dives:** [Best NMN 2026](/supplements/best-nmn-supplements-2024) · [NAD+ boosters guide](/supplements/nad-boosters-complete-guide) · [NMN vs NR](/compare/nmn-vs-nr)  
**Database:** [NMN](/database/nmn)

### 6. Taurine — 78/100

Large observational + emerging interventional interest for metabolic and cardiovascular markers. Excellent safety; easy Tier 1 add-on in our stack model.

**Deep dive:** [Best taurine supplements](/supplements/best-taurine-supplements)  
**Database:** [Taurine](/database/taurine)

### 7. Ashwagandha (KSM-66) — 74/100

Strongest adaptogen data for stress and sleep quality. Useful when cortisol and recovery are the bottleneck — not a magic longevity pill.

**Deep dive:** [Best ashwagandha](/supplements/best-ashwagandha-supplements)  
**Database:** [KSM-66](/database/ashwagandha-ksm66)

### 8. Urolithin A — 73/100

Rare case of a "trendy" compound with multiple human RCTs on muscle endurance and mitochondrial markers (Mitopure pathway).

**Deep dive:** [Urolithin A / Mitopure guide](/supplements/urolithin-a-mitopure-guide)  
**Database:** [Urolithin A](/database/urolithin-a-mitopure)

### 9. Berberine — 72/100

Metabolic tool for glucose and lipids when lifestyle is not enough. GI tolerance varies; not for everyone.

**Deep dives:** [Best berberine](/supplements/best-berberine-supplements) · [Berberine vs metformin](/supplements/berberine-vs-metformin)  
**Database:** [Berberine HCl](/database/berberine-hcl)

### 10. CoQ10 / Ubiquinol — 70/100

Most useful when statin use, heart failure risk, or high training load create a clear need. Prefer ubiquinol for older adults.

**Deep dive:** [Best CoQ10 / ubiquinol](/supplements/best-coq10-ubiquinol)
